# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are technical-control and expertise professionals preparing mission work for existing buildings or classified establishments in Luxembourg. They use the product before field or review work to understand case-file evidence, missing mission-critical documents, uncertainty, and next actions.

Users are expert, time-constrained, and accountable. They need a reviewable preparation dossier that supports professional judgement without pretending to replace it.

## Product Purpose

Building Mission Readiness converts public planning sources, demo site profiles, lightweight geospatial context, uploaded documents, and system-derived missing-information records into a structured mission preparation dossier.

The product helps users select a demo mission case, review site context, upload case documents, generate a readiness dossier, inspect evidence behind findings, and open source files. Success means the expert can quickly see what is available, what is missing, what requires validation, and what to request, inspect, measure, scan, verify, or control next.

The system must preserve the distinction between evidence availability and final engineering, legal, structural, environmental, fire-safety, ITM, SNSFP, AEV, HVAC, compliance, occupancy, or energy-certification conclusions.

## Brand Personality

Precise, sober, accountable.

The interface should feel like a trustworthy technical workspace: calm under complexity, explicit about uncertainty, and respectful of expert judgement. It should project institutional competence rather than startup spectacle.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic RAG chatbot patterns, conversational answer boxes, glossy AI assistant framing, decorative dashboards, compliance theater, and visual language that implies certification, approval, safety, or legal certainty.

Avoid over-styled SaaS tropes: gradient heroes, inflated metrics, excessive cards, dramatic dark themes, decorative motion, and color used as ornament rather than evidence state.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence before narrative: findings, checklist items, and readiness states should stay visibly connected to their source records.
2. Uncertainty is a first-class state: missing, partial, unknown, and not applicable must be legible without sounding like failure.
3. Expert control remains central: the product prepares decisions, it does not make final technical or legal judgements.
4. Dense but reviewable: screens should support scanning many records while preserving hierarchy and traceability.
5. Local Luxembourg specificity matters: labels, examples, and flows should reflect mission preparation in this regulatory and building-context domain.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, and form labeling. Status should not rely on color alone; text labels must accompany semantic states. Motion should be minimal and respect reduced-motion preferences. Long source names, multilingual terminology, and technical evidence text must wrap or truncate predictably without blocking access to the underlying content.
